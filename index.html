About

Before Trapezoid, I was a research scientist at Meili Technologies, a Cornell Tech spinout detecting in-vehicle medical emergencies like seizures and cardiac events. My PhD, in Data Science at the University of Washington, was on sequential decision-making under uncertainty; I also hold a master's in Computer Science & Systems Engineering from UW and a bachelor's from the Technion.

That thread runs through what I do now: post-training frontier models, designing the reward signals and evaluations that shape their behavior, and the alignment work that keeps them reliable.

I'm a selected member of the Transportation Research Board's ACP-70 committee, part of the National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine. I also care about science communication: I've worked on several outreach projects and won first place in the University of Washington's university-wide Three Minute Thesis competition, along with a Diversity, Equity & Inclusion Award, a Grace Hopper Celebration Scholarship, and a Data Science for Social Good Fellowship.
